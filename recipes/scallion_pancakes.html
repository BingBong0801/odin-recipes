Scallion Pancakes

Descriptions

Scallion pancakes are a popular snack and street food in China and other parts of Asia, and can also be found in many Chinese restaurants around the world. They are a delicious and easy-to-make dish that can be enjoyed as a snack, appetizer, or side dish, and are a great way to use up extra scallions in your kitchen.

Ingredients

  • 2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 cup boiling water
  • 1/2 cup thinly sliced scallions (green onions)
  • 1/4 cup vegetable oil

Instructions

  1. In a large mixing bowl, whisk together the flour and salt.

  2. Slowly add the boiling water to the flour mixture, stirring with chopsticks or a wooden spoon until a rough dough forms.

  3. Turn the dough out onto a lightly floured surface and knead for 5-7 minutes until smooth and elastic.

  4. Cover the dough with a damp towel and let it rest for 30 minutes.

  5. After the dough has rested, divide it into 4 equal pieces and roll each piece into a thin, round pancake (about 8 inches in diameter).

  6. Brush the top of each pancake with vegetable oil and sprinkle with sliced scallions.

  7. Roll up each pancake tightly, jelly-roll style, and then coil each roll into a spiral shape, tucking the end underneath.

  8. Use a rolling pin to flatten each spiral into a thin pancake (about 1/4 inch thick).

  9. Heat a nonstick pan over medium heat and add 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil.

  10. Once the oil is hot, add a pancake to the pan and cook for 2-3 minutes on each side, until golden brown and crispy.

  11. Remove the pancake from the pan and place it on a paper towel-lined plate to cool.

  12. Repeat with the remaining pancakes, adding more oil to the pan as needed.

  13. Serve the scallion pancakes warm, cut into wedges, with soy sauce or another dipping sauce of your choice.
